1. what are Candlesticks:

Candlesticks are visual representations of price movements in financial charts, widely used in technical analysis. Each candlestick conveys information about the opening, closing, high, and low prices during a specific time period, helping traders analyze market sentiment and potential trends.

2. What is a Doji Candlestick:

A Doji is a candlestick pattern formed in financial charts when the opening and closing prices are very close or nearly identical, resulting in a small or nonexistent body. Visually, it resembles a cross or plus sign, indicating market indecision and potential trend reversal. Dojis are essential in technical analysis, providing insights into shifts in market sentiment.

Learn More

3. What is a Marubozu Candlestick:

A Marubozu is a Japanese candlestick pattern characterized by a candle with little to no wicks or shadows, representing a strong and decisive price movement during a trading period. The absence of upper and lower wicks signifies that either buyers (bulls) or sellers (bears) dominated the entire session.

Learn More

4. Spinning Tops:

Spinning tops are candlesticks with small bodies and both upper and lower wicks of roughly equal length. They suggest indecision in the market, with neither buyers nor sellers dominating the trading session.

5. What is a Dual Candlestick:

Dual candlestick patterns involve two consecutive candlesticks that, when analyzed together, provide insights into potential market reversals or continuations. Traders use these patterns for technical analysis to make informed decisions about future price movements.

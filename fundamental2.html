Understanding financial ratios

What are financial ratios?

Financial ratios are quantitative metrics used to assess a company's financial performance, health, and efficiency. They provide valuable insights into various aspects of a company's operations, allowing analysts and investors to make informed decisions.

Importance of financial ratios

1. Financial ratios are crucial for understanding a company's financial health.
2. They help in comparing different companies in the same industry and making informed investment decisions.
3. Financial ratios offer entrepreneurs a way to evaluate their company's performance and compare it other similar businesses in their industry.

Candlestick vs Line Graph

Candlesticks: Provide more information, depicting open, high, low, and close prices. Useful for analyzing market sentiment and potential trend reversals.
Line Graphs: Show only closing prices over time. Simplifies data representation, useful for observing overall price trends but lacks details present in candlesticks.

Types of financial ratios

1. Liquidity Ratios

a. Liquidity ratios measure a company's ability to pay off its short-term liabilities.
b. Example: Current ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities.

2. Profitability Ratios

a. Profitability ratios measure a company's ability to generate earnings relative to its expenses and other costs.
b. Example: Return on Assets (ROA), Return on Equity (ROE).

3. Solvency Ratios:

a. Assess a company's ability to meet long-term obligations and the extent to which it is financed by debt.
b. Example: Debt-to-Equity Ratio, Interest Coverage Ratio.

4. Valuation Ratios:

a. Provide insights into the market's perception of a company's value.
b. Example: Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio ,Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio.

5. Growth Ratios:

a. Indicate the rate at which a company is growing in terms of revenue or earnings.
b. Example: Earnings Growth Rate, Revenue Growth Rate.

6. Coverage Ratios:

a. Assess a company's ability to cover various financial obligations.
b. Example:Times Interest Earned, Dividend Coverage Ratio.

7. Efficiency Ratios

a. Efficiency ratios measure how well a company uses its assets and liabilities internally.
b. Example:Asset turnover ratio, Inventory turnover ratio.

Calculating financial ratios

Steps to Calculate Financial Ratios:-

1. Identify relevant financial data from the financial statements.
2. Utilize the appropriate formula to calculate the desired ratio.

Interpreting financial ratios

How to Interpret Financial Ratios?

1. Understand the significance of high or low ratio values and compare calculated ratios with industry standards to gain insights into a company's performance.
2. Consider broader economic trends and market conditions when interpreting ratios for a comprehensive analysis.
3. Interpretation involves not only numerical analysis but also a qualitative understanding of the company's unique context.

Understand this with example:

Question1: ABC Company has the following financial information:

a. Current Assets: $150,000
b. Current Liabilities: $75,000

Calculate the Current Ratio.

Solution 1: The formula for the Current Ratio is: Current Ratio = Current Assets /Current Liabilities

Now, let's substitute the given values into the formula:

Current Ratio = $150,000

$75,000

Current Ratio=2

RESULT: The Current Ratio for ABC Company is 2. This means that for every dollar of current liabilities, the company has $2 in current assets. The Current Ratio is a measure of a company's short-term liquidity, indicating its ability to cover its short-term obligations. In this case, a Current Ratio of 2 suggests that ABC Company has a healthy liquidity position.

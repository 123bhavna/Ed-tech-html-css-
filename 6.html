What is reversal

What is reversal

1. Reversal in trading signifies a shift in the prevailing trend direction.
2. Bullish reversals indicate a change from a downtrend to an uptrend, while bearish reversals suggest a transition from an uptrend to a downtrend.

Engulfing Patterns:

An engulfing pattern is a dual candlestick pattern in technical analysis where the second candle fully covers or "engulfs" the body of the preceding one, indicating a potential trend reversal.


1. Bullish Engulfing Pattern: The second candle (bullish) completely engulfs the body of the first (bearish), signaling a potential reversal from bearish to bullish sentiment. It often indicates strong buying interest.
2. Bearish Engulfing Pattern: The second candle (bearish) completely engulfs the body of the first (bullish), suggesting a potential reversal from bullish to bearish sentiment. It indicates strong selling interest.

Harami Pattern

Harami candlestick pattern involves a smaller candle nestled within the body of a larger one, signaling potential trend reversal


Tips to recognize-

1. Look at the previous candle, the longer one, it depicts the mother.
2. Note the small candle, which is within the real body of the previous candle, is the baby.
3. Ignore the color of small candle.
4. If this pattern occurs after an uptrend within a long positive candle, it is a top reversal.

Harami Pattern

Trading implications -

1. Potential Reversals: It signals a potential trend reversal, especially after a downtrend.
2. Confirmation: Look for confirmation through subsequent bullish price action to validate the reversal signal.
4. Context Matters: Bullish Harami is more significant in a broader downtrend.
